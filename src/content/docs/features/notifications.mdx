## How a brief is delivered

When you send a brief:

1. **Render** — we compile the brief content + branding + recipient list.
2. **Submit to SendGrid** — our email infrastructure provider handles the actual SMTP delivery.
3. **Per-recipient logging** — we store a SendGrid message ID for each recipient so we can correlate later events (opens, clicks, bounces) back to this specific send.
4. **Portal token issued** — every brief gets a signed portal URL embedded in the email and a per-contact hub URL.

The full sequence completes in 10-60 seconds. Recipients see the email within 30 seconds in most cases (subject to their mail provider's delivery speed).

## What recipients see

### In their inbox
A branded HTML email with the brief content inline. The "View in portal" link at the top opens the hosted version.

### In the hosted portal
Two flavors:

- **Single-brief portal**: `app.sendbriefs.com/portal/r/[token]`. Opens the specific brief in a branded web view.
- **Contact hub portal**: `app.sendbriefs.com/portal/c/[contact-token]`. Lists every brief sent to that contact, on one page.

Both support PDF download. Recipients don't need to log in — the token in the URL grants read access.

> **Security model.** Portal URLs are bearer tokens. Anyone with the URL can view that brief. For clients with strict confidentiality needs, set the access tier to `verified` (requires a 6-digit code emailed to the contact's address before the portal loads). See [Clients → portal access](/features/clients/#stricter-portal-access).

## Tracking what happens after send

Each recipient has four signals on the brief's engagement bar (see [Reports & data](/features/reports/) for the full breakdown):

- **Delivered** — SendGrid accepted and forwarded. Reliable.
- **Opened** — tracking pixel loaded. Best-effort (privacy filters mask this).
- **Clicked** — any tracked link clicked. Stronger signal.
- **Portal views** — recipient opened the brief in the portal. Cleanest signal.

Plus failure states:

- **Bounced** — recipient's mail server rejected the message.
- **Marked as spam** — recipient or filter marked the brief as spam.
- **Failed** — couldn't submit to SendGrid at all (rare, indicates a workspace config issue).

## Customizing the email itself

Two settings tabs shape what your recipients see in their inbox:

### Email Layout (Settings → Reports → Email Layout)

The **email wrapper template** — what wraps your brief content when it's emailed out. Subject prefix, header content, footer content, and the visual chrome around the rendered brief.

Open the **Email Layout** tab to see the workspace's templates as a card grid. You can have multiple (e.g., a default + a specific one for VIP clients) and pick which one to use per brief.

### Email Settings (Settings → Delivery → Email Settings)

Sender setup — from-name, from-address, reply-to. Also where suppression-list management lives (recipients who've bounced hard or marked the workspace's mail as spam are suppressed; you can view and manage that list here).

For custom-domain sending (briefs going out from `reports@youragency.com` instead of `reports@sendbriefs.com`), the configuration lives here. Custom-domain setup may not be fully self-serve in the current preview — talk to support if it's blocking.

## Internal notifications

You and your team get notified about activity inside SendBriefs — brief lifecycle, section work, team changes, mentions.

### Event types

| Event | Preference label | Triggers | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| `report_submitted` | Reports submitted for approval | A brief moves to `pending_approval` | Live |
| `report_approved` | Your reports approved | Your brief is approved | Live |
| `report_rejected` | Changes requested on your reports | A reviewer asks for edits | Live |
| `report_sent` | Your reports sent | Your approved brief is emailed to the client | Live |
| `section_reminder` | Deadline reminders | An assigned section is incomplete near a deadline | Live |
| `section_assigned` | Sections assigned to you | Colleague assigns a section to you | Listed but no producer wired yet |
| `section_comment` | Comments on your sections | Someone comments on a section you own | Listed but no producer wired yet |
| `member_joined` | New team members | Someone new joins your workspace | Live |
| `mention` | Mentions | A teammate @-mentions you | Listed but no producer wired yet |

> Three events appear in your preference list today but won't fire in the current preview because their producers aren't yet wired in the product (per code comments in the standalone notifications page). Tune them as you like — they're harmless until the producers ship.

### Channels

Each event type has two independent toggles: **in-app** (the bell icon in the header + your `/notifications` page) and **email** (sent to your account address). There's also a **Test** button per row that fires a sample notification so you can confirm a channel is working.

Defaults are tuned for solo operators — in-app on for most things, email reserved for approvals and direct assignments. For larger teams, you'll want to tune this per person to reduce noise.

Change preferences from **Settings → Delivery → Notifications** (or your personal `/notifications` page).

### The bell icon

The header bell shows unread in-app notifications. Click to see the list; click any notification to jump to the relevant brief or section. The unread count updates in real time as new notifications arrive.

## What you can't get yet

Setting honest expectations on what's *not* in the product today:

- **No Slack notifications for brief activity.** Slack has an OAuth connect flow in the Integrations card and credentials are stored when you complete it, but no code in the current preview sends messages to Slack — not for notifications, not for digest, not for activity summaries. In-app and email are the only working channels.
- **No SMS or push notifications.** Email + in-app are the only channels.
- **No digest mode.** Notifications fire per event. If you want a quiet workspace, turn off email for routine events (`report_sent`, `section_comment`) and keep only the ones that demand attention.
- **No custom event subscriptions.** You subscribe at the workspace level (every brief from any client), not per-client or per-template.

## Unsubscribe and compliance

Every brief includes an unsubscribe link in the footer (required by anti-spam law).

When a recipient unsubscribes:

1. They land on a confirmation page.
2. They're added to a suppression list.
3. We won't send future briefs to that address from this workspace.
4. The workspace owner is notified.

You can't disable the unsubscribe link — it's a legal requirement.

## Common gotchas

- **Open rates lie.** Apple Mail Privacy Protection pre-loads tracking pixels via a proxy, inflating opens to ~95-100% for iPhone users. Read clicks and portal views as the real engagement signal.
- **Bounced contacts stay bounced.** A hard bounce suppresses the contact. To re-enable, update their email on the client record. Adding the same email back without changes won't re-enable delivery.
- **Connecting Slack doesn't do anything yet.** The OAuth flow completes and shows "Connected," but no product feature uses the connection in the current preview. Watch this space.
- **Notification preferences are per-user, per-workspace.** If you belong to multiple workspaces, tune each one separately.
