## Workspace settings

Open **Settings** in the sidebar. The settings page has **5 groups, 16 tabs** total. Most workspace-management work happens in these:

**Workspace group:**

- **General** — workspace name, agency details, default layout mode (`strict` / `flexible`).
- **Branding** — logo, primary + accent colors.
- **Team** — invite members, change roles, remove members.

**Reports group:**

- **Templates** — the saved brief templates this workspace uses.
- **Approvals** — per-workspace approval policy.
- **Scheduling** — recurring brief schedules (per-client, frequency, time).
- **Email Layout** — the email wrapper template (subject, header, footer) used when briefs send.

**Delivery group:**

- **Email Settings** — custom-domain / sender setup.
- **Media Library** — shared images and assets referenced by templates.
- **Notifications** — per-event in-app and email toggles for the signed-in user.

**Account group:**

- **Client Portal** — portal customization (per-workspace).
- **Security** — workspace-wide MFA enforcement.
- **Billing** — subscription management via the Stripe Customer Portal.
- **Integrations** — OAuth connections + Brief integrations mappings.

**Admin group:**

- **Logging** — redirects to the [Activity page](#audit-log--activity) where the audit log lives.
- **Danger Zone** — workspace deletion (Owners only).

The tabs you see depend on your role: most settings require `settings:update` permission (Owner or Admin); Billing requires `billing:read`; Danger Zone requires `workspace:delete`.

## Member roles

| Role | Can do | Can't do |
|---|---|---|
| **Owner** | Everything: manage members, billing, integrations, workspace settings. Delete the workspace. | n/a |
| **Admin** | Everything Owner can do except delete the workspace. | Delete workspace. |
| **Member** | Create and edit briefs they own. Read briefs from teammates. Read clients, contacts, and the team directory. | Manage billing, team, or workspace settings. Edit briefs they don't own. |

Roles are configured per-workspace; the same person can be an Owner in one workspace and a Member in another.

> There's a fourth role internally called **Client** — but that's for portal recipients, not for human team members. You don't assign it. Clients view briefs through a signed link, not a login. See [Clients](/features/clients/).

## Inviting members

From **Settings → Team**, click **Invite member**:

1. Enter email — one at a time.
2. Pick a role (Owner, Admin, Member).
3. Send.

The invitee gets an email with a sign-up link valid for **7 days**. If they miss it, resend from the same screen — the old token is revoked.

If they're already a SendBriefs user, they'll switch into the workspace from their account menu. Otherwise they go through signup first, then land in the workspace.

## Removing or downgrading members

From **Settings → Team**, click a member's row:

- **Change role** — promote or demote.
- **Remove from workspace** — they lose access immediately. Their previously-authored briefs stay; you can't delete a Member's contribution after the fact.

Owners can't be removed by Admins — only another Owner (or self-removal after transferring ownership) can do that.

## Transferring ownership

There must be at least one Owner. To hand off:

1. Promote the new person to **Owner** from their row.
2. Demote yourself to **Admin** or **Member** (or remove yourself).

You can't reduce the workspace to zero Owners. The UI will refuse.

## Multi-factor authentication

Two layers:

### Per-user MFA
Any user can enable TOTP-based MFA for themselves from the sidebar's **Profile → Security** page (route: `/profile/security`). Note: this lives on the dedicated Profile page, not inside Settings. Strongly recommended for anyone with Owner or Admin access.

First-time enrollment (when no MFA factor exists yet) also has a top-level flow at `/mfa-enroll` — Settings → Account → Security can require this for everyone in the workspace.

### Workspace-enforced MFA
Owners can require every member to have MFA enabled. From **Settings → Account → Security**, flip on **Require MFA**.

Once enforced:

- Existing members who don't have MFA enabled will be required to enroll TOTP at next login before they can do anything else.
- New invitees go through the same gate on first login.

This is a one-way switch from a security standpoint — turning it off again later is allowed, but members who enrolled stay enrolled (they have to disable per-user).

## Branding

Workspace branding has three knobs:

- **Logo** — PNG, JPG, or SVG. Square works best (renders at 32 × 32 in the nav, scales up for brief headers).
- **Primary color** — used for CTAs, links, the active-state highlight. Default `#6366f1` (indigo).
- **Accent color** — used for gradient accents and decorative chrome. Default `#8b5cf6` (violet).

Edit from **Settings → Branding** (in the Workspace group). Changes apply immediately to new briefs; previously-sent briefs keep their rendered state (history is frozen).

### Per-client branding
Per-client logo is supported (set on the client record). Per-client colors are not — clients inherit workspace colors. If you have white-label workflows with multiple agency sub-brands, run them in separate workspaces.

## Multi-workspace pattern

You'd want multiple workspaces if:

- You operate **two distinct agency brands** that don't share clients or staff.
- You need **hard data isolation** for compliance (e.g., healthcare clients in their own workspace with stricter access).
- You run an **agency network** where each franchise has its own data, members, and billing.

Each workspace is fully separate: separate billing, separate members, separate client list, separate integrations. Briefs and clients don't cross.

To switch workspaces, click the workspace name at the top of the sidebar to open `/workspaces` and pick a different one. (If you only belong to one, this is a no-op view.)

> Most agencies need exactly one workspace. If you're not sure whether you need a second, you don't.

## Audit log / Activity

Every action that matters is logged: member changes, integration connect/disconnect, brief send events, role changes, billing changes.

The audit log surfaces on the **Activity** page in the sidebar (`/activity`), with an **Advanced** tab for the full event stream. Owners and Admins can filter and export.

Settings → Admin → Logging is a pointer that redirects to `/activity?tab=advanced` — same destination.

## Common gotchas

- **There's no Editor or Viewer role.** If you're used to other tools, the SendBriefs model is just Owner / Admin / Member. Permissions are coarser — Members get edit access to their own briefs by default, not viewer-only access.
- **You can't move briefs between workspaces.** Briefs are workspace-scoped. If you need to migrate, save the brief as a template, then re-create it in the destination workspace from the template.
- **Removed members can't be un-removed automatically.** Re-invite them by email — they'll go through the standard invite flow.
- **Workspace deletion is hard.** Only an Owner can delete (Settings → Admin → Danger Zone), and the action is two-step with confirmation. All briefs, clients, and history go with it. There's no undo.
