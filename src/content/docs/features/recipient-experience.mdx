## What recipients see in their inbox

When a brief sends, each recipient gets one of these:

- **Individual mode** — a personal email; only their address is in `To:`. Each recipient gets a personalized portal link.
- **Shared mode** — all recipients on the `To:` line; one shared portal link goes to everyone.
- **Auto mode (default)** — single recipient → individual; 2+ recipients → shared. The workspace setting can be overridden per client or per brief.

The email itself is the brief content rendered inline (via the email layout you chose), wrapped in your branding:

- **From:** the workspace sender name + address (e.g., `Acme Reports <reports@acme.com>`). Templates can override these unless workspace email settings are locked.
- **Subject:** typically `{client name} — {brief title}` (configurable per email layout).
- **Body:** the rendered brief, plus the layout's greeting, signature, and footer blocks.
- **CTA button:** "View in portal" — opens the hosted portal page.
- **Footer:** "Sent via SendBriefs" (suppressed if the **White-label** add-on is active), plus a per-recipient unsubscribe link required by anti-spam law.

## The hosted portal

The portal is the same content as the email, but rendered in a branded web view with PDF download and (for sections-based briefs) a table of contents. It's hosted under the SendBriefs app domain (or your custom domain if the **Custom domain** add-on is active).

Three portal URLs exist:

### `/portal/r/[token]` — individual brief link
The per-recipient token. Each recipient gets a unique one. Opens that recipient's view of one specific brief. Tracks view counts per recipient.

Recipients with this URL can:

- Read the rendered brief in the browser.
- Download as PDF.
- Print.
- Click through any links in the content.

They **cannot** edit, comment, see other recipients, or browse other briefs.

### `/portal/s/[token]` — shared brief link
The shared-mode token. Same recipient experience as `/r/`, but the same link works for everyone CC'd on the brief (no per-recipient binding). Useful when distribution mode is `shared` and you'd rather have one URL.

### `/portal/c/[contactToken]` — contact hub
The hub for one specific contact. Lists every brief ever sent to them, in reverse-chronological order. Each entry links into the brief's individual portal view.

This URL is included in every brief sent to that contact, so they can navigate their own history. The hub is the closest thing to a "client login" the product has — but it's still token-based, not password-based.

> **Security model.** Portal URLs are bearer tokens. If a recipient forwards the link, anyone with the URL has the same access. Treat portal URLs the way you'd treat a Dropbox share link. For sensitive content, use the `verified` access tier (below).

## Portal access tiers

The **Settings → Client Portal** tab sets the workspace-wide default tier. The tier picker is gated to **Pro plan or higher** — free/starter/trial workspaces see a locked card and stay on the default tier.

### `public`
The token in the URL is enough. No further verification.

- Cleanest UX for recipients.
- Right for low-sensitivity reporting.

### `signed` (the default)
Token is signed and expiring. After the configured expiry (7 / 30 / 90 / 365 days, or never), the portal page becomes inaccessible — recipients see `/portal/expired` instead.

- Each recipient gets a unique signed link.
- Right for most agencies — better than `public`, no extra friction for recipients.

### `verified`
Highest tier. Recipients must prove they control the email the brief was sent to before the portal renders.

The mechanism — **one method**, no toggles:

1. Recipient clicks their portal link.
2. The verify card appears: "To access this report, please verify your email address." (The recipient's email is looked up server-side from the contact record — they don't type it.)
3. Recipient clicks **Send verification code**.
4. They receive a plain-text email with subject "Your verification code: 123456" and a 6-digit numeric code that expires in **10 minutes**.
5. Recipient enters the code (6 digits, single-use, max 5 attempts before they need a new code).
6. On success, a signed cookie is set that grants access to that specific token for **24 hours**. After 24 hours, they verify again.

Right for board-facing reports, regulated industries, or any client with explicit confidentiality requirements.

> **Two things worth knowing about verified tier:**
>
> - **Per-client override schema exists, but there's no UI yet to set it.** The workspace-wide default is what applies; per-client overrides are honored by the access code but not yet writeable from the client detail page. Use the workspace default for now.
> - **Shared-mode tokens cannot be verified-tier.** Verified only works with per-recipient tokens (the contact has to be identifiable). Shared sends fall back to `signed`.

## Recipient-side actions and non-actions

Recipients can:

- Read the brief content.
- Download a PDF.
- Print.
- Open links inside the brief.
- (In contact hub) browse their own history of sent briefs.
- Unsubscribe — removes them from future sends in this workspace.

Recipients **cannot**:

- Edit any content.
- Comment on the brief or send feedback through the portal (there's no client-side comment surface in the current preview).
- Log in as a user (the `CLIENT` role exists for portal sessions but has no dashboard access).
- Access other clients' briefs.
- See contributor names or internal metadata.
- Download source data (only the rendered PDF).

If they want to ask a question or request changes, they reply to the email — that goes to the workspace sender address.

## Expired links

A token expires when:

- Its time-based expiry passes (`signed` or `verified` tiers with non-`never` expiry).
- A `signed` single-use token has been used.
- The workspace is deleted or suspended (cancelled subscription past data-retention window).

The recipient sees `/portal/expired` — a simple page that explains the link is no longer valid and points them to your workspace email for help. They can't self-serve a new link.

If you need to re-issue access:

- Re-send the brief from the sent brief's menu. The new send generates fresh tokens.
- Or for a contact hub link, ask support for a re-issue (no self-serve UI yet).

## Bounces, unsubscribes, and suppression

The recipient's inbox is the source of truth on whether they actually got the brief. The brief detail page surfaces what happened — but a few things happen automatically:

- **Hard bounce** (invalid address, mailbox doesn't exist) — the contact is auto-suppressed via SendGrid's event webhook. They won't get future briefs until you manually restore them.
- **Spam complaint** — the contact is auto-suppressed. Same restoration path.
- **Soft bounce** (mailbox full, temporary failure) — no suppression; SendGrid retries.
- **Unsubscribe** — the recipient clicks the unsubscribe link in the email footer. They're added to the suppression list, scoped to the workspace.

Suppressed contacts are visible in the contacts list with a "Suppressed" badge. To restore, click the contact → restore from the suppression list.

## Branding the recipient view

Two branding layers can apply to the recipient view:

- **Workspace branding** — logo, primary + accent colors, typography (Settings → Branding). This is the default for any client that doesn't have its own branding.
- **Per-client branding** — set a logo, primary color, accent color, brand mark, brand typography, and custom subdomain on the client record. When set, these override workspace branding for that client's briefs and portal.

> **White-label note.** The "Sent via SendBriefs" footer text is suppressed when the **White-label** add-on is active on the workspace. The **Custom domain** add-on lets the portal serve from your own subdomain. Both add-ons are bundled with the Enterprise tier; à la carte on lower tiers.

## Common gotchas

- **Recipients don't see contributor names.** Even if three people contributed to the brief, the recipient sees one cohesive brief from the workspace sender.
- **The contact hub link is a token, not a login.** If a recipient bookmarks the hub URL, anyone with that URL can see all their briefs. Don't share contact hub URLs broadly.
- **Verified-tier codes go to the exact email on the contact record.** If a contact forwards the portal link to a colleague, the colleague won't be able to verify — they'd need the verification email, which goes to the original recipient.
- **Soft bounces don't suppress.** If briefs keep failing to a "mailbox full" address, manually flip `receive reports` off on that contact — SendGrid won't auto-suppress them.
