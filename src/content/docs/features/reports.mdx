## What you can see about a sent brief

Open any brief in the `sent` state. Two surfaces summarise what happened:

- **Engagement bar** (top of the brief) — small pills showing cadence, comment count, open count, open rate, and "sent X ago."
- **Delivery card** (below the brief content) — recipient-by-recipient table with Delivered / Opened icons per row, plus aggregate Recipients / Delivered / Opened totals.

The signals you can read:

### Delivered
SendGrid accepted the email and forwarded it to the recipient's mail server. Usually logged within 30 seconds of send.

**Reliable.** If "Delivered" is green, the message reached the mail server. Whether the user reads it is a different question.

### Opened
The recipient's mail client loaded our 1×1 tracking pixel.

**Best-effort.** Privacy filters significantly distort this number:

- **Apple Mail Privacy Protection** (default on iOS 15+ and macOS Monterey+) auto-loads images via a proxy, inflating opens.
- **Outlook with image-blocking** never triggers an open.
- **Plain-text recipients** never trigger an open.

Don't read too much into "0 opens." Use it as one of several signals, not as the answer.

### Clicked
The recipient clicked any tracked link in the brief (links are rewritten to route through our click tracker, including the portal CTA).

**Stronger signal than opens.** Clicks require intent. Clicked events are captured per recipient in the email-event trail, though they don't currently render as a separate column in the recipient list — view the per-recipient detail to see them.

### Portal views (data only — not in UI yet)
When a recipient opens the brief in the hosted portal, the portal token's `view_count` and `last_viewed_at` increment. This is the cleanest engagement signal (it can't be triggered by mail-client preloading), but it isn't surfaced in the brief detail UI in the current preview. It lives in the database and is accessible via export.

## Bounces, failures, and unsubscribes

The underlying email-events trail captures problems too — they surface in the per-recipient delivery detail:

- **Bounced** — recipient's mail server rejected the message (invalid address, mailbox full, blocked sender). Hard bounces auto-suppress the contact via SendGrid's webhook; the contact won't get future briefs until you manually restore them. Soft bounces don't suppress.
- **Failed** — we couldn't even submit to SendGrid. Rare; usually a workspace-level config issue. Email support if you see this.
- **Marked as spam** — the recipient flagged the brief as spam, OR their mail provider's filter did. Triggers auto-suppression. Repeated spam markings hurt your sender reputation.

## The email-events trail

Behind the scenes, every SendGrid event (delivered, opened, clicked, bounced, spam-reported, unsubscribed) is recorded with a timestamp. Open the brief's **Engagement** detail to see the full chronological log per recipient.

For most agencies, the summary in the engagement bar is enough. The detailed log matters when you're troubleshooting (e.g., "did this brief actually go to all five contacts last quarter?").

## Workspace-level analytics

The dashboard has an **Email analytics** card that rolls up the last 30 days across every brief:

- Total briefs sent
- Open rate
- Click rate
- Bounce rate

It's a quick health check — useful for spotting trends (a drop in opens may signal a deliverability issue, an unusually high bounce rate may signal a list-hygiene problem).

## Data inside the brief (the other side of "reports")

The numbers your client sees in a brief — sessions, revenue, conversions, whatever — come from connected integrations.

Where those numbers come from in the current preview:

- **Available now**: **ClickUp** — via the Brief integrations system, which auto-pulls items from a ClickUp list into the matching brief template's draft.
- **Connect-only**: Google and Slack — OAuth flows complete but no data adapters yet.
- **Coming soon**: HubSpot, Stripe, GA4 direct, Google Search Console, Google Ads, Meta Ads, Linear, PostHog, BigQuery, Calendly, generic webhook.

See [Integrations](/features/integrations/) for the full picture, setup steps, and per-source status.

## What doesn't exist yet

To set expectations honestly:

- **No cross-source metric normalization.** "Revenue" from one source isn't auto-mapped to "revenue" from another. Each data block binds to one specific integration field.
- **No custom-formula metrics.** You can't yet define `ROAS = revenue / ad_spend` as a workspace metric.
- **No bulk data export.** If you need a CSV of all brief engagement across all clients, email support and we'll pull it for you.

These are all on the roadmap. None are blockers for the standard "monthly client report" workflow.

## Common gotchas

- **Apple Mail Privacy Protection skews opens up.** A 100% open rate with 5% click rate often means most of the "opens" are Apple's proxy.
- **Tracking pixel blocked = no open recorded.** Recipients using Mailtrack-style extensions or strict spam filters can read the brief without ever triggering "Opened."
- **Portal views aren't surfaced in the UI yet.** Even though they're tracked in the database, the brief detail page doesn't yet show portal view counts per recipient. If you need them, ask support for an export.
- **One recipient, one mail client.** If someone reads on phone (opens) and then desktop (opens again), you'll see two open events. The first-open timestamp is the most reliable signal.
