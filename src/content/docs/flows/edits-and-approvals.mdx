## When approvals are worth the friction

Approval workflows trade speed for safety. They're worth it when:

- The client is a **major account** where a mistake would cost the relationship.
- The data is **high-stakes** (board-facing, regulatory, financial reporting).
- You're **early in a relationship** and want extra eyes on the first 3-6 sends.
- The **brief includes prose commentary** that benefits from a second editor's read.

For routine work — established clients, well-tested templates, low-stakes commentary — send-without-approval and rely on the Friday review habit to catch issues.

## How internal approval works

The brief state machine has four primary states plus one side state:

```
draft ───► pending_approval ───► approved ───► sent
              │      ▲
              ▼      │
         changes_requested
```

### Configuring the workspace policy

Approval behavior is configured at **Settings → Reports → Approvals**. You can:

- Set a **default policy** for the whole workspace (e.g., "all briefs require approval before send").
- Create **per-client overrides** (e.g., "Acme briefs skip approval, others require it"). The settings page has a client dropdown for adding overrides.
- Toggle approvals off entirely — briefs go straight from `draft` to `sent` when you click Send.

Owners and Admins have access to this tab (`settings:update` permission).

### Submitting for approval

When a brief is ready, click **Submit for approval** in the editor (instead of **Send**). The brief moves to `pending_approval`. Designated approvers get a `report_submitted` notification (in-app + email by default).

### Approver experience

The approver opens the brief from their notification. They see the full rendered preview and can:

- **Approve** — brief moves to `approved`. From here, you or any sender can move it to `sent`.
- **Request changes** — brief moves to `changes_requested` with the approver's note. The author gets a notification.

After **Request changes**, the brief is back with the author. They fix what was flagged, submit again, and it returns to `pending_approval`.

### Who can approve

Approval authority follows workspace roles:

| Role | Can approve? |
|---|---|
| **Owner** | Yes |
| **Admin** | Yes |
| **Member** | No (unless the workspace explicitly assigns approver duties to a Member) |

There's no per-client approver assignment in the current preview. If you need that level of granularity (e.g., "Sam approves all Acme briefs"), the closest path today is: assign Sam as an Admin, give them sole responsibility for Acme briefs by convention, and rely on the workspace MFA + audit trail for accountability.

### Reverse transitions

Briefs can also move backwards through the state machine:

- `sent → draft` — you noticed an error after send and need to fix and resend.
- `approved → draft` — approver caught something between approval and send.
- `pending_approval → draft` — author wants to pull it back before review.

These are deliberate "I changed my mind" paths. Each transition is logged.

## Self-approval

If you're the only person in the workspace and approvals are required, you'll still need to click Approve on your own brief before send.

Most solo workspaces just leave approvals off — the friction isn't worth it when you're the only reviewer.

## What's not in the product today

Setting expectations honestly. These are common asks but aren't built yet:

### External (client-side) approval

There's no built-in "the client must approve before send" workflow. If a client wants to review briefs in advance, the manual workaround is:

1. Use **Send test to me** to render the brief.
2. Forward to the client with "preview — let me know if changes needed."
3. Edit if needed; send the final via SendBriefs.

When external approval ships, this page will document it.

### Bulk approve

Each brief in `pending_approval` is approved one at a time today. If you have 20 briefs queued and they all look fine, that's 20 clicks. For very large rosters, ask support — there's a CLI path that can handle bulk approve as a one-off.

### Approval deadlines

There's no "auto-approve after N hours of no response" today. Approvers either approve or the brief sits in `pending_approval` indefinitely. If a brief gets stuck, the author can transition it back to `draft` and reassign.

### Approval comments per block

Approvers leave one comment per "request changes" action. There's no inline-per-block comment thread yet. For more granular feedback, use the request-changes note to point at specific blocks ("the revenue table in section 3 looks off").

## Handling client edit requests after send

Sometimes a client replies after a brief has shipped: "this number is wrong" or "can you also include X?"

### Option A: send a corrected version manually

1. Open the brief.
2. Move it back to `draft` (brief menu → **Move to draft**).
3. Fix the issue.
4. Add a clear note in the email body explaining the correction.
5. Send.

The corrected brief is a new send event — recipients get a fresh email, the engagement bar starts over for this send.

> **Heads up:** the brief's history will show the previous `sent` state and the new send. Both versions remain in the audit trail.

### Option B: fix it for next period

Lower-friction. Make a note in the brief's comment thread (or your own notes): "Next period: fix the conversion source." Update the binding for next month. Move on.

Most agencies prefer (B) unless the error materially misrepresents the period. Correction sends create more noise than they fix for minor issues.

## Audit trail

Every state transition on a brief is logged with timestamp + actor:

- Created
- Submitted for approval
- Approved (with approver identity)
- Changes requested (with approver note)
- Sent (with recipient list and timestamp)
- Reverted to draft (with reason if provided)

Open the brief's **History** to see the full chronology. For workspace-wide audit reporting (e.g., compliance reviews), the audit log is accessible to Owners and Admins from the workspace settings.

## Multi-author briefs

Templates can mark sections as assigned to specific contributors. When a brief is created from such a template, the assigned contributor gets notified to fill in their section. Section assignment is template-level — you configure it once in the Template editor, not on every brief.

Per-section review state (section-level approve / request-changes independent of the brief as a whole) isn't surfaced in the UI in the current preview. Review still happens at the **whole-brief** level: the author submits the entire brief for approval once everyone's contributions are in.

> Earlier versions of this page described a section-status state machine (`to do` / `in progress` / `done` / per-section `approved`). That schema was retired when sections moved into the unified block editor — the legacy section-status enum was dropped. Sections are now just structured blocks. Review their content visually; approve at the brief level.

## Common gotchas

- **Approval policy is workspace-wide.** You can't yet require approval on some templates and skip it on others. The closest path is to use the brief state machine manually — submit for approval when you want review, send directly when you don't.
- **The author can't be the approver in practice.** Owners and Admins can technically approve their own briefs, but the audit trail will flag a single-person workflow. For solo workspaces, just leave approvals off rather than performing review theater.
- **Reverting `sent → draft` doesn't unsend the email.** The brief in your dashboard is back to editable, but the original send is in the recipient's inbox. If you need to address what already went out, send a corrected version (see Option A above).
- **`changes_requested` keeps the brief editable for the author.** The approver's note appears as an in-app notification + email. You don't need to reset state manually — just edit and resubmit.
