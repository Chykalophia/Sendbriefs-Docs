## The painful version (what we're replacing)

Without SendBriefs, the typical monthly cycle:

1. **Day 1 of the month**: log into 3-5 platforms per client, copy numbers into a Google Doc.
2. **Days 2-4**: format, polish, write commentary, fix the formatting again.
3. **Day 5**: send manually, often with last-minute fixes.
4. **Days 5-7**: chase the one client who replies "can you also include X?"
5. **Repeat for every client.** Twenty clients × 2 hours × 12 months = 480 hours per year on report assembly alone.

## The SendBriefs version

After the initial setup, the cycle:

1. **Friday before send day**: open the queued briefs for the upcoming send. Skim each preview. Approve or flag for edits. ~10 minutes for a roster of 20 clients.
2. **Send day**: briefs go out on schedule. The engagement bar fills in as recipients open and click.
3. **Through the week**: respond to actual client questions about the *content* — not the formatting.

If you do this for a year: ~60 hours of active reporting work instead of 480. The reclaimed time goes to client strategy, new business, or — radically — taking Fridays off.

## Setup: do this once

### Build your house template

Start from a brief that worked well (yours or a starter). Click **Save as template** from the brief menu. Name it durably — "Monthly performance — Studio v2."

The template remembers the block structure, cadence (set this to `monthly`), and section layout. Per-client data values are NOT saved — they re-resolve each period from the connected integrations.

[Templating a recurring brief →](/flows/templating-recurring/) covers this in detail.

### Per-client briefs from the template

For each client:

1. **Reports → New brief → From template → [your template]**.
2. Set the **client** to the one you're onboarding into the cycle.
3. Set the **reporting period** to "last calendar month" (or whatever your standard is).
4. Save as draft.

You now have a draft brief per client, all using the same template.

### Move briefs to the recurring cycle

For each draft, set it on a recurring schedule from the brief detail page:

- **Cadence**: monthly on day 1, 2, or 3 (later in the month gives integrations time to settle their final numbers).
- **Send time**: 9–10 am client-local.
- **Approval policy**: optional. If your workspace requires approvals, the brief will move to `pending_approval` ahead of the scheduled send.

> **Heads up on scheduling.** Recurring sends are governed by a feature flag in the current preview. If you don't see the "Set recurring" option on a draft, the feature isn't enabled for your workspace yet — email support to flip it on. In the meantime, the manual path is to create each period's brief from the template on send day.

## The monthly cycle (after setup)

### Friday before send week: review

The previous month's data is now available. Open **Reports** and look at the briefs scheduled for the upcoming Monday.

For each:

1. Click to open the brief.
2. Skim the numbers. Anything that looks wildly off (10× anomaly, missing data warning)?
3. If something looks wrong: open the underlying data, decide if it's a real issue or a binding glitch.
4. If something is genuinely interesting (a record high or low, a campaign that worked): add a one-line commentary to the prose block.
5. Approve (if your workspace requires approval), or just leave it scheduled.

For 20 clients, this takes about 10 minutes. The whole point is that *most* months are routine — the data is right and the brief reads itself.

### Send day: ignore everything (almost)

Briefs send automatically on their scheduled time. You'll see `report_sent` notifications (in-app, plus email if you've left email on for that event) as each one ships.

If a brief fails to send (integration broken, data block returned an error), you'll see a notification immediately. Reconnect the integration in **Settings → Integrations** and the next render will catch up.

### Through the week: respond to substance

Most replies are positive — "thanks, looks great." Some will be substantive — "wait, why did organic traffic drop?"

These are the conversations you want to be in. They're not formatting questions; they're strategy questions. SendBriefs gives you back the time to answer them well.

## What to do when it goes wrong

### Integration broken during a batch

If GA4 tokens expired or Slack disconnected, the affected briefs will fail their next render. Reconnect from **Settings → Integrations**, then re-run the brief. The notification system will surface the failure; you don't have to babysit.

### A client wants a one-off addition

Open their next scheduled brief. Add the requested block or write commentary in a prose block. Save. The recurring schedule continues unchanged — your edit applies only to this period.

If they want this every month, add it to the template instead.

### A client wants a different cadence

Edit the recurring schedule on their specific brief. The change is per-client, not template-wide.

### You want to pause everything

Each recurring brief can be paused from its detail page. For a workspace-wide pause (around holidays, transitions, etc.), there's no single "pause all" toggle — you'd pause each client's recurring brief individually. If you have a large roster, email support and we can handle this in one operation.

## A sample weekly rhythm

How one pilot agency (15 clients, 2-person team) runs it:

| Day | Activity | Time |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon AM** | Skim `report_sent` notifications from Sunday-night batch. | 3 min |
| **Mon-Wed** | Substantive client replies. | Variable |
| **Thu** | Onboard new client (if any). Test template changes. | 30 min |
| **Fri PM** | Review next week's queued briefs. Approve. | 10 min |
| **Sat-Sun** | Off. | 0 min |

The Friday review is the load-bearing habit. Treat it like a 10-minute calendar event. If you skip it, the briefs still send — but you lose the opportunity to add per-period commentary that turns a report into a conversation.

## What's not in the product yet

To be honest about gaps in the current preview:

- **No bulk-schedule UI.** You set the schedule per draft. For 5-10 clients this is fine; for 50 clients, ask support about a one-time bulk setup.
- **No Slack digest of sent briefs.** Slack notifications for brief activity aren't wired up yet (the Slack integration exists for other purposes). You'll see `report_sent` notifications in-app and over email.
- **No automatic correction-send.** If a brief shipped with bad data, the path is: fix the underlying issue, manually resend from the brief menu, and add a note in the email body explaining the correction.
