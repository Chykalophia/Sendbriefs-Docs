<p class="sb-tldr-label">TL;DR</p>
SendBriefs meters three things at the workspace level: <strong>active users</strong>, <strong>active clients</strong>, and <strong>reports sent this month</strong>. Plan limits trigger when you try to <em>add</em> a new one. No surprise charges — just a polite "upgrade or remove something" prompt. Usage shows on the Billing tab.
</aside>

## What's counted

Three meters run in the background. You can see current values on **Settings → Billing**.

### Active users
The number of unique members in the workspace. Counts Owner, Admin, and Member roles. Doesn't count `CLIENT` (those are portal recipients, not real users).

- Pending invitations don't count until accepted.
- Removed members stop counting immediately.
- The user count is point-in-time, not a running total.

### Active clients
The number of clients in the workspace that aren't archived. Archived clients don't count; reactivating one brings it back into the count.

Inactive clients still count. Only **archived** clients are excluded.

### Reports sent this month
Count of briefs that reached the `sent` state during the current calendar month.

- Resends of the same brief (sent → draft → re-send) count each send as a separate event.
- Test sends (like the Email Layout "Email myself" preview) don't count, because they send placeholder content rather than a real brief.

## How limits are enforced

When you try to **add** something — invite a new member, create a new client, send a brief — SendBriefs checks the current count against your plan's limit:

- **Under the limit:** action proceeds normally.
- **At the limit:** the action is refused with a polite prompt and an **Upgrade** button. No surprise charge.
- **Over the limit (due to plan downgrade):** existing items keep working, but you can't add new ones. You can either upgrade back or remove some to fit.

## When you can't downgrade

Downgrades fail if your current usage exceeds the lower tier's limits. Example: you're on Agency with 30 clients; trying to downgrade to Solo (which allows 5 clients) is refused. The Stripe Customer Portal surfaces this — archive clients first, then try the downgrade again.

## Why your Stripe invoice may not match what you see here

Usage is reported to Stripe monthly. There's a lag of up to a day between SendBriefs' usage view and Stripe's billing view.

For day-to-day decisions, trust SendBriefs' Billing page. For the invoice itself, trust the Stripe-issued invoice.

## What's NOT metered

- **Brief drafts.** You can create unlimited drafts. Only the send counts.
- **Templates.** No template count limit in the current preview.
- **Contacts.** Unlimited per client; no workspace cap.
- **Integrations.** Unlimited connections (subject to Stripe's add-on charges if you've purchased White-label or Custom domain).
- **Scheduled reports.** No cap on the number of schedules per workspace.
- **Audit log entries.** Generated freely; retention drives eventual purge.

## Trial-tier limits

During the 14-day free trial, the workspace runs at the trial-tier limits. For current numbers, check **[sendbriefs.com/pricing](https://sendbriefs.com/pricing)** — we don't quote them here so this page stays accurate as the trial tuning evolves.

A few Pro-gated features (e.g., the `verified` portal access tier) are locked on the trial; everything else is available. If you hit a trial limit, the dashboard surfaces an "Upgrade" prompt. You can upgrade before the 14 days end if you've outgrown the trial.

## At day 15

When the trial expires without a paid plan:

- The workspace transitions to a **suspended** state.
- **Briefs stop sending.** Active schedules are paused.
- **Data is preserved.** Templates, clients, contacts, sent brief history — all intact.
- You can still log in and view, but can't create new briefs.

To resume: **Settings → Billing → Choose a plan** and complete Stripe Checkout. The workspace reactivates when the first charge succeeds.

## Common gotchas

- **Inactive clients still count.** Only `archived` is "free." If you have lots of paused clients, archive them rather than just marking inactive to free up plan quota.
- **Resends DO count.** If you're at the monthly limit, a quick "resend with correction" still consumes one send. (Test sends — the Email Layout "Email myself" preview — don't count.)
- **Plan downgrades don't auto-revoke add-ons.** White-label and Custom domain stay active (and billable) even after downgrade, unless you explicitly cancel them in the Stripe Customer Portal.
- **The monthly send counter resets on calendar month boundaries**, not subscription anniversaries. A workspace that started its subscription on the 15th still resets the send counter on the 1st.
