<aside class="sb-tldr">
<p class="sb-tldr-label">TL;DR</p>
Four plans: <strong>Solo</strong> (1-3 clients, single user), <strong>Studio</strong> (4-25 clients, up to 5 team members), <strong>Agency</strong> (26-100 clients, up to 20 team members), <strong>Enterprise</strong> (custom). All plans include the core platform; higher tiers unlock approvals, scoping, client portals, and SSO.
</aside>

For current pricing, see [sendbriefs.com/pricing](https://sendbriefs.com/pricing). This page describes what each tier includes; we don't hardcode dollar amounts here because they change.

## How to pick a plan

Pick based on the **constraint that bites first**:

- If you'll exceed the **client limit** in 6 months, size up.
- If you'll exceed the **team member limit** in 6 months, size up.
- If you need a **specific feature** (approvals, SSO, client portal), size up to the tier that includes it.
- If none of the above, start one tier lower and upgrade when needed.

## Solo

For founders and consultants with 1-3 long-term clients.

**Includes:**
- All core features
- 1 team member (you)
- Internal approval (e.g., a virtual assistant approving before send)
- 30 days of audit log retention

**You'll outgrow this when:**
- You add a 4th client
- You hire someone

**Most users on Solo:** independent consultants on retainer with 1-2 anchor clients.

## Studio

The most popular plan. For 2-5 person agencies running 5-25 client engagements.

**Includes:**
- Everything in Solo, plus:
- Up to 5 team members
- External client approval workflow
- 90 days of audit log retention
- White-label sending (custom domain + branding)
- Priority email support (4-hour response)

**You'll outgrow this when:**
- You hit 25 clients
- Your team grows past 5 people
- A client requires a custom contract / SSO

**Most users on Studio:** boutique agencies, fractional CMO firms, specialized service agencies (paid-media-only, SEO-only, etc.).

## Agency

For larger agencies with multiple service lines and client-scoped role requirements.

**Includes:**
- Everything in Studio, plus:
- Up to 100 clients
- Up to 20 team members
- Client-scoped roles (e.g., "Alex only sees her assigned clients")
- Client portal (recipients can log in to see history)
- SSO via SAML or OIDC
- 1 year audit log retention
- Slack-shared workspace channel for support

**You'll outgrow this when:**
- You hit 100 clients
- You need custom contract terms
- You have specific compliance requirements (HIPAA, FINRA)

## Enterprise

Custom contracts. Volume discounts. Compliance support. Dedicated CSM.

Talk to sales: [sendbriefs.com/contact-sales](https://sendbriefs.com/contact-sales).

**Typical features:**
- Unlimited clients and team members
- Custom integrations
- Custom data retention
- HIPAA BAA available
- SOC 2 reports available
- Custom SLA
- Dedicated infrastructure (US/EU/UK regions)

## Annual vs. monthly billing

Annual saves ~17% (effectively 2 free months). Annual is paid upfront.

For most agencies, annual is the right call once you're past the 14-day trial and confident this is the platform you'll stay on. The savings compound over the contract length.

## Add-ons

A few features are priced per-use rather than per-plan:

- **Extra audit log retention** — beyond plan default
- **Custom integrations** — if we don't support a source you need
- **Onboarding services** — for agencies migrating from another platform

Talk to sales for any of these.

## Free trial

14 days. No credit card. Full feature access at Agency-tier limits.

After 14 days, your workspace pauses (briefs stop sending) until you pick a plan. You don't lose data — your templates, clients, and integrations stay intact.

## What happens if you exceed plan limits

- **Soft cap (clients/team members):** you can keep adding until you hit a hard cap that's typically 20% over your plan. Once you hit the hard cap, we'll prompt you to upgrade.
- **No surprise charges.** We won't auto-charge you for going over. If you need to add a client and you're at the limit, the upgrade prompt will be obvious.
