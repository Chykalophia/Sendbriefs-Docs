## What add-ons are

Add-ons are paid entitlements that live alongside your subscription plan. Unlike plan tiers (which gate big chunks of functionality and client/team limits), add-ons toggle specific brand-presentation features.

Two add-ons exist today:

- **White-label** (`white_label`).
- **Custom domain** (`custom_domain`).

Both are workspace-scoped: enabling them affects every brief and portal page in the workspace.

## White-label

**What it changes:**

- The **"Sent via SendBriefs"** footer line is removed from outgoing emails.
- SendBriefs branding is removed from portal headers and footers.
- The recipient experience appears entirely as your agency.

**What it doesn't change:**

- The portal URL is still on `app.sendbriefs.com` unless you also have the Custom domain add-on.
- The "from" address is still your workspace email (e.g., `reports@sendbriefs.com` or whatever's configured at Settings → Email Settings) — White-label doesn't change sender domains.
- Internal app pages (everything inside the dashboard) still show SendBriefs branding to logged-in users.

White-label is the right add-on for agencies that want to fully white-label the **recipient-facing** surface without using their own domain.

## Custom domain

**What it changes:**

- The recipient portal is served from your subdomain — e.g., `briefs.youragency.com` instead of `app.sendbriefs.com/portal/...`.
- Portal URLs embedded in emails now use the custom subdomain.
- Combined with White-label, the recipient experience has no SendBriefs surface area at all.

**What you need to set up:**

- A subdomain you control (typically `briefs.` or `reports.` of your domain).
- A DNS record (CNAME) pointing the subdomain at SendBriefs' portal.
- TLS certificate provisioning (automatic once DNS resolves correctly).

**The current setup path:** purchase the add-on in Settings → Billing, then **email support with your desired subdomain**. Custom domain DNS configuration isn't fully self-serve yet — support wires up the routing and certificate after you confirm the DNS record.

## Pricing and bundling

- **Enterprise tier:** both add-ons are bundled — no extra charge.
- **Paid plans below Enterprise:** both are à la carte. Pricing visible in Settings → Billing → Add-ons (subject to the same Founders Circle preview pricing caveats as plans themselves).
- **Trial / free tier:** add-ons are not purchasable on these tiers — upgrade to a paid plan first.

When you upgrade to Enterprise, any à la carte add-ons you previously bought become bundled (and you stop paying separately for them).

## How to purchase

1. **Settings → Billing → Add-ons** section.
2. Click **Purchase** on the add-on you want.
3. Stripe Checkout opens with the prorated charge.
4. Confirm.
5. The add-on activates immediately (no waiting for the next billing cycle).

Both add-ons are recurring — they renew with your subscription.

## How to cancel

1. **Settings → Billing → Manage Subscription** → opens Stripe Customer Portal.
2. Under your subscription, remove the add-on line item.
3. Confirm.

Removal takes effect at the end of the current billing cycle (you keep the add-on for the rest of the period you paid for).

## What happens to old briefs when an add-on is added or removed

Briefs already sent are **frozen** with the branding state at the moment they shipped:

- A brief sent before you bought White-label still shows "Sent via SendBriefs" in the recipient's inbox (the email was already delivered).
- The portal view for that brief, however, re-renders on each visit — so it picks up the new White-label state.
- A brief sent before you set up a Custom domain still has the original `app.sendbriefs.com` portal URL embedded in the recipient's email. New sends will use the custom subdomain.

So White-label flips both ways immediately for the portal; Custom domain flips only for new sends.

## Common gotchas

- **Custom domain requires DNS access.** If you don't control DNS for the subdomain, you can't set this up. Plan around your IT team's lead time.
- **Removing an add-on doesn't auto-revoke for in-flight briefs.** If you cancel Custom domain mid-cycle, briefs in `draft` or `pending_approval` will still try to send with the custom domain in the recipient email until the cancellation takes effect at cycle end.
- **Plan downgrades don't auto-cancel add-ons.** If you downgrade from a tier where add-ons were bundled to one where they're à la carte, you'll start being charged for them separately. Cancel them in the Customer Portal if you don't want that.
- **White-label is workspace-wide.** You can't toggle it on for one client and off for another. For that pattern, run different agency brands in separate workspaces.
