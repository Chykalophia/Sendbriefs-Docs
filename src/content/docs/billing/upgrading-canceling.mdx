<aside class="sb-tldr">
<p class="sb-tldr-label">TL;DR</p>
Upgrades are immediate and prorated. Downgrades take effect at the end of the current cycle. Cancellation stops the subscription at cycle end; afterwards the workspace transitions to suspended (briefs stop, data preserved). All these changes route through the Stripe Customer Portal.
</aside>

## Upgrading

Upgrades take effect immediately. You're charged the prorated difference for the remainder of the current cycle.

### How to upgrade

1. **Settings → Billing → Manage Subscription**.
2. The Stripe Customer Portal opens.
3. Click **Update plan** (or the equivalent CTA on the portal).
4. Pick the new tier.
5. Stripe shows the prorated charge.
6. Confirm.

The upgrade reflects immediately in SendBriefs — gated features become available within seconds.

### Proration math

If you upgrade mid-cycle:

- Stripe credits the unused portion of your current plan for the rest of the cycle.
- Stripe charges the prorated cost of the new plan for the same period.
- The difference is the amount on your next charge.

You can also see the proration preview in the Customer Portal before confirming.

## Downgrading

Downgrades take effect **at the end of the current billing cycle**, not immediately. You don't lose features you've already paid for.

### How to downgrade

Same path: **Settings → Billing → Manage Subscription → Update plan → pick a lower tier**.

You'll see a notice: "Plan will downgrade to [Plan] at the end of the current cycle." Until that date, you have your current plan's features.

### Hitting the new plan's limits

You can't downgrade to a plan whose limits you currently exceed. For example, if you have 30 clients on the Agency plan and try to downgrade to Solo (which allows ~5 clients), the change will be rejected.

The portal will surface this. Either archive enough clients to fit under the lower plan's limit, or stay on your current tier.

## Canceling

Canceling stops your subscription at the end of the current billing cycle. You keep access until then.

### How to cancel

1. **Settings → Billing → Manage Subscription**.
2. Click **Cancel subscription** (or the equivalent CTA).
3. Stripe asks for confirmation and (optionally) a reason.
4. Confirm.

You'll get a cancellation confirmation email. The subscription remains active until the end of the current cycle.

### Canceling mid-cycle

You keep access through the end of the cycle you've already paid for. We don't refund the unused portion automatically — for that, see the [refund policy](#refunds-policy) below.

### What happens after the cancellation date

When the cycle ends and the cancellation takes effect:

1. **Briefs stop sending.** Any scheduled briefs are paused.
2. **The workspace transitions to a suspended state.** You can still log in and see everything, but you can't create new briefs or send. Data is preserved.
3. **You can reactivate at any time** by picking a plan again (see below).

> **Data retention after cancellation** isn't explicitly documented in the preview. If you're cancelling and need a specific data-retention commitment, talk to support before you cancel.

## Reactivating after cancellation

To come back after canceling:

1. Log in to your existing account.
2. The workspace shows a "Subscription required" banner.
3. **Settings → Billing → Choose a plan** (or click the banner).
4. Pick a plan, complete Stripe Checkout, confirm.

If you're reactivating within a short window, your data should be intact — templates, clients, integrations, audit logs. Any briefs that *would* have sent during the suspended window are not auto-sent; you decide whether to send them now or skip.

## Switching billing cadence (monthly ↔ annual)

To switch from monthly to annual:

1. **Settings → Billing → Manage Subscription**.
2. Pick the annual variant of your current plan.
3. Stripe prorates the unused monthly portion as a credit toward the annual charge.
4. Confirm.

To switch from annual back to monthly, the change happens at your annual renewal date — we don't break annual contracts mid-cycle.

## Refunds policy

Refund handling depends on the situation:

- **First 30 days of annual**: typically refundable on request. Email support.
- **Monthly plan cancellations**: no refund of the in-progress cycle; the cycle plays out and then ends.
- **Mid-annual cancellations**: handled case-by-case. Email support and we'll work through the specifics.
- **Mistaken charges** (e.g., you canceled and were still charged): emailed to support, resolved within 1 business day.

## Exporting data before canceling

If you're canceling and want a clean copy of your data:

- **Brief content** — each brief in `sent` state has a portal URL you can save (PDF download is available from the portal).
- **Templates** — currently in-app only; no JSON export in the preview. If you need a copy, email support.
- **Client list** — currently in-app only; no CSV export in the preview. Email support for a one-time export.
- **Audit log** — Owners and Admins can request a CSV export from support.

Once data export is more first-class in the product, we'll update this page.

## What happens to your clients' data

Cancellation only affects *your* workspace. Briefs already in your clients' inboxes stay there — they're not retracted. Portal links remain accessible until the portal tokens expire (or until your workspace data is purged per retention policy, whichever comes first).

We **don't** automatically email your clients on your behalf when you cancel. That's your call.

## What's not in the product today

- **In-app plan change UI.** All plan changes route through Stripe's Customer Portal. We surface a button; Stripe handles the flow.
- **Self-serve bulk data export.** No "download everything as a ZIP" button. Email support for a workspace-wide export.
- **Auto-notify clients on cancellation.** No built-in client-facing comms when you wind down. Manual outreach is your responsibility.

## Common gotchas

- **Downgrade gotcha:** if you're on Agency with 20 team members and try to downgrade to Solo, you'll need to remove most members first.
- **Annual cancellation timing:** canceling on day 360 of a 365-day annual cycle means you have 5 more days of access, then it ends.
- **Reactivation is account-level, not workspace-level.** Your user account stays usable for other workspaces even if a specific workspace is suspended.
- **You can have multiple cancellations and reactivations.** No penalty for changing your mind, but the data retention clock starts each time.
