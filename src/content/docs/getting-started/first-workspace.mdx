## What a workspace is

A workspace is your agency's container. Inside it lives:

- **Clients** — the organizations you report to.
- **Briefs** — your reports and templates. (App sidebar calls them *Reports*.)
- **Members** — your team (roles: Owner, Admin, Member).
- **Integrations** — data sources connected at the workspace level.
- **Billing** — the subscription paying for it all.

Most agencies need exactly one workspace. You'd only want multiple if you operate two distinct brands that don't share clients or team members.

## The onboarding wizard

When you land in the app right after signup, you'll see a 4-step wizard. You can click **Skip** at the bottom of any step and finish it later from **Settings**.

### Step 1 — Tell us about you

Three quick questions:

- **Your role** — Agency Owner, Project Manager, or Team Member. Used to tune the dashboard hints; doesn't change permissions.
- **How many clients** — 1–5, 6–20, or 20+. Used to recommend a starting plan when your trial ends.
- **Agency name** — what your workspace will be called. Editable later from **Settings → Workspace**.

### Step 2 — Upload your logo

PNG, JPG, or SVG. Square works best (we display at 32 × 32 in the nav and scale to the brief header).

Skip if you don't have one yet. You can upload from **Settings → Branding** any time.

### Step 3 — Pick brand colors

Two hex inputs with color pickers:

- **Primary** — the main brand color. Used for CTAs, links, the highlighted state of nav items. Default: `#6366f1` (indigo).
- **Accent** — the secondary brand color. Used for gradient accents and section dividers. Default: `#8b5cf6` (violet).

These show up in your client portal and (when configured) in branded briefs. If you don't know what to pick, the defaults are fine — they're the SendBriefs brand and look clean on a white surface.

### Step 4 — Add your first client

- **Client name** — required.
- **Primary contact email** — optional, but we recommend filling it now so the wizard wires up a complete example.

We'll go deep on clients on the next page. For now, the wizard creates a minimal client record so the dashboard isn't empty when you land.

Click **Finish** and you'll be dropped on the dashboard. The Onboarding checklist card stays visible until everything's wired — you can come back to any of these steps from there.

## What happens after the wizard

You'll see the dashboard with:

- The onboarding checklist showing what's done and what's left.
- A product tour highlighting where to find Reports, Clients, Settings, and the Comfort Panel.
- An empty Recent Briefs feed (it'll fill in once you send one).

The wizard does NOT cover team invites or billing — those have their own dedicated screens, and most agencies don't need either on day one.

## Inviting your team

Open **Settings → Team** and click **Invite member**. Add their email and pick a role:

| Role | What they can do | What they can't do |
|---|---|---|
| **Owner** (`WORKSPACE_OWNER`) | Everything. Manage billing. Delete the workspace. | n/a |
| **Admin** (`ADMIN`) | Everything except delete the workspace. | Delete workspace |
| **Member** (`MEMBER`) | Create and edit briefs they own. Read briefs from teammates. Read clients and team directory. | Manage billing, team, or workspace settings |

Invitations are valid for **7 days**. If someone misses theirs, resend from the same screen.

Roles are editable later from **Settings → Team** if you need to promote or demote someone.

> SendBriefs has a fourth role (`CLIENT`) internally — but it's not invitable. It's the stored role for portal recipients who view briefs through a signed link rather than logging in. You'll never assign it.

## Multi-factor authentication (optional, recommended)

If you want everyone in the workspace to use 2FA, an Owner can turn this on from **Settings → Account → Security**. Once enforced, members are routed to `/mfa-enroll` at next login and required to enroll TOTP before they can do anything else in the app.

For the personal MFA setup flow, see [Create your account → Turn on two-factor authentication](/getting-started/create-account/#turn-on-two-factor-authentication).

## Pick a plan (when the trial ends)

The 14-day trial unlocks everything. Don't pick a plan now unless you're sure.

When you're ready, go to **Settings → Billing** to start a Stripe Checkout session. See [Billing & plans](/billing/plans/) for what each tier includes.
