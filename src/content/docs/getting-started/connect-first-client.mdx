## What a client is in SendBriefs

A "client" is the organization that receives your briefs. Each client record holds:

- **Basic profile** — name, optional website, optional logo.
- **Contacts** — the actual people on the client side. One is marked Primary; others can be Billing, Stakeholder, or Technical. Each contact has a `receive reports` toggle so you can list someone as a stakeholder without spamming them.
- **Status** — active, inactive, or archived. Lets you keep historical clients findable without cluttering the active list.

Clients **do not log in**. When a brief is sent, recipients get an email with a signed link to a hosted client portal — no password required.

## Adding the client

Open **Clients → New client** in the sidebar (or hit the **Add client** button on the dashboard).

<Placeholder type="screenshot" caption="New-client form with name, website, logo fields" />

You'll see:

| Field | Required? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Client name** | Yes | What you call them internally. Shows up everywhere in the app. |
| **Website** | No | Helps you orient when you have many clients with similar names. |
| **Logo** | No | Used in the client portal header and on white-label briefs. PNG, JPG, or SVG. Square works best. |
| **Primary contact name** | No (recommended) | The default "Send to" person. |
| **Primary contact email** | No (recommended) | Same. Required before you can actually send a brief to this client. |

Click **Create client**. You'll land on the client detail page.

## Adding contacts

You can add as many contacts as you need. Each contact has:

- **Name + email** — required.
- **Role** — one of `Primary`, `Billing`, `Stakeholder`, `Technical`. Used for organization; doesn't change permissions.
- **Receive reports** — toggle. On = this person gets briefs when you send. Off = they're listed on the account but kept off the recipient list.

From the client detail page, click **Contacts → Add contact** and fill in the form.

<Placeholder type="screenshot" caption="Contact form with role selector and 'receive reports' toggle" />

A few patterns worth noting:

- **One Primary, many Stakeholders.** Most agencies have one main day-to-day contact (Primary) and a handful of executives or specialists who want to see what's going on (Stakeholders with `receive reports` on).
- **Billing contacts** typically have `receive reports` off — they only need to see things when something invoice-related happens.
- **Technical contacts** are the people who handle the data connections (the ones who own the GA4 property, for example). Often `receive reports` off.

## Connecting data sources

Briefs render from data. To get any meaningful number into a brief, you need at least one **integration** wired up.

Important: integrations are connected **at the workspace level**, not per-client. One OAuth to Google covers every Google property you can access; the per-client choice happens when you bind a data block inside the brief.

Open **Settings → Integrations** to see what's available.

<Placeholder type="screenshot" caption="Integrations page with available providers grid" />

### Available today

- **Google** — connects your Google account for Analytics (read-only) and profile data. One OAuth covers every GA4 property the connected Google account has access to.
- **Slack** — connects to a Slack workspace for posting brief activity to a channel.

### Coming soon

These are on the roadmap and will appear in the same place when they ship. None are wired up yet — don't plan a brief around them.

- **HubSpot** — CRM data (companies, deals, pipeline).
- **Stripe** — revenue, subscriptions, MRR.
- **Google Search Console** — organic search performance.
- **Google Ads** — paid search performance.
- **Meta Ads** — Facebook and Instagram campaigns.
- **Linear** — project status and shipped work.
- **PostHog** — product analytics.
- **BigQuery** — direct warehouse access for advanced setups.
- **Calendly** — meetings booked and held.
- **Webhook** — generic inbound for anything we don't natively integrate.

Full per-integration setup steps live in [Integrations →](/features/integrations/).

### Connecting Google (the typical first integration)

1. From **Settings → Integrations**, click **Connect Google**.
2. You'll redirect to Google's OAuth consent screen. Sign in with the Google account that has access to your client's GA4 property.
3. Grant the read-only Analytics scope. We never request write.
4. You'll bounce back to SendBriefs. The integration shows as **Connected** with the email of the signed-in Google account.
5. The first brief you build for this client will let you pick which GA4 property to pull from.

> **If the client's GA4 property isn't visible later**, the Google account you signed in with doesn't have access. Get a viewer-level role added to that GA4 property, then either reconnect or pull the property again. The simplest pattern: ask the client to add your agency's shared Google account as a Viewer in their GA4.

## Common gotchas

- **OAuth tokens expire.** Most providers issue refresh tokens. We refresh in the background and show a status warning if a token actually fails. Reconnect from **Settings → Integrations** if you see a red banner on the integration.
- **One Google account, many properties.** Connect once; pick the property per-client when you bind data in the brief editor.
- **No integration ≠ no brief.** You can write a brief entirely in the block editor without any integration connected — just no live numbers. Useful for narrative-only updates.
