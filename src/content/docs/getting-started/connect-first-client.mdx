## What a client is in SendBriefs

A "client" is the organization that receives your briefs. Each client record holds:

- **Basic profile** — name, optional website, optional logo.
- **Contacts** — the actual people on the client side. One is marked Primary; others can be Billing, Stakeholder, or Technical. Each contact has a `receive reports` toggle so you can list someone as a stakeholder without spamming them.
- **Status** — active, inactive, or archived. Lets you keep historical clients findable without cluttering the active list.

Clients **do not log in**. When a brief is sent, recipients get an email with a signed link to a hosted client portal — no password required.

## Adding the client

Open **Clients → New client** in the sidebar (or hit the **Add client** button on the dashboard).

<Placeholder type="screenshot" caption="New-client form with name, website, logo fields" />

You'll see:

| Field | Required? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Client name** | Yes | What you call them internally. Shows up everywhere in the app. |
| **Website** | No | Helps you orient when you have many clients with similar names. |
| **Logo** | No | Used in the client portal header and on white-label briefs. PNG, JPG, or SVG. Square works best. |
| **Primary contact name** | No (recommended) | The default "Send to" person. |
| **Primary contact email** | No (recommended) | Same. Required before you can actually send a brief to this client. |

Click **Create client**. You'll land on the client detail page.

## Adding contacts

You can add as many contacts as you need. Each contact has:

- **Name + email** — required.
- **Role** — one of `Primary`, `Billing`, `Stakeholder`, `Technical`. Used for organization; doesn't change permissions.
- **Receive reports** — toggle. On = this person gets briefs when you send. Off = they're listed on the account but kept off the recipient list.

From the client detail page, click **Contacts → Add contact** and fill in the form.

<Placeholder type="screenshot" caption="Contact form with role selector and 'receive reports' toggle" />

A few patterns worth noting:

- **One Primary, many Stakeholders.** Most agencies have one main day-to-day contact (Primary) and a handful of executives or specialists who want to see what's going on (Stakeholders with `receive reports` on).
- **Billing contacts** typically have `receive reports` off — they only need to see things when something invoice-related happens.
- **Technical contacts** are the people who handle the data connections (the ones who own the GA4 property, for example). Often `receive reports` off.

## Connecting data sources (optional in the current preview)

Briefs are written in the block editor — you can ship a brief with no integrations connected at all. Most content (text, headings, tables, callouts) is written directly.

For automated content — auto-pulling items from an external tool into a brief's General section — there's a **Brief integrations** system under **Settings → Integrations → Brief integrations**. In the current preview, that supports **ClickUp** (configured with an API token + a ClickUp list ID, mapped to a brief template).

> **Setting expectations honestly.** Several integrations on the marketing site (HubSpot, Stripe, Google Analytics, Google Ads, Meta Ads, etc.) aren't wired up yet. **Google** and **Slack** each have an OAuth connect flow in the **Settings → Integrations** card and the flow completes, but no functional data pipeline uses the stored credentials today. Treat them as placeholder connections for features in development.

For the full integration story — what works, what's connection-only, and what's roadmap — see [Integrations →](/features/integrations/).

## Common gotchas

- **You don't need an integration to ship a brief.** The block editor + your prose is enough for most monthly briefs in the current preview. Add integrations only when you want auto-ingest of external content.
- **The Integrations card shows Google and Slack as "available."** Those OAuth flows complete and store credentials, but the product doesn't yet pull data from Google or send messages to Slack. The credentials sit there for features in development.
- **ClickUp is the working integration today.** If your team tracks brief inputs in ClickUp, the Brief integrations system auto-appends new list items into the matching draft brief.
