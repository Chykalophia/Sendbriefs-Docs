From <strong>Reports → New brief</strong>, pick a template (or start blank). Fill out the blocks in the editor. Optional: assign sections to teammates. Submit for approval if your workspace requires it, otherwise go straight to send. Preview, send a test to yourself, then send to the client.
</aside>

## The mental model

A brief is a stack of **blocks** assembled in a rich editor. Blocks include text, headings, tables, charts, images, callouts, and data tokens that pull live numbers from your connected integrations.

Each brief has a **status** that moves through this state machine:

```
draft → pending_approval → approved → sent
              ↓                  ↑
       changes_requested ────────┘
```

If your workspace doesn't require approval, briefs go directly from `draft` to `sent`. You can move a brief backwards too (e.g., `sent → draft` to fix something and resend).

Two more concepts:

- **Templates** — reusable brief structures with a defined cadence (weekly, monthly, quarterly, etc.). Save any brief as a template and reuse it for every client.
- **Sections** — collapsible groups of blocks. Each section has its own status (`to do`, `in progress`, `done`, `additional notes`) and can be assigned to a teammate. Useful when multiple people contribute to one brief.

## Step 1: Create the brief

From the sidebar, click **Reports → New brief**.

<Placeholder type="screenshot" caption="New-brief modal with 'From template' and 'Start blank' options" />

You'll be asked:

- **Title** — what this brief is called internally. The client may see this in the email subject.
- **Client** — pick from your client list (the one you set up on the [previous page](/getting-started/connect-first-client/)).
- **Template** — pick a starter template or start blank. For your first brief, "blank" is often fastest — you can save it as a template after.
- **Reporting period** — the time window this brief covers. Used by data tokens that ask "give me last month's numbers."

Click **Create**. You'll land in the editor.

## Step 2: Write in the block editor

<Placeholder type="screenshot" caption="Block editor with a text block, a chart block, and a data-token block visible" />

The editor works like Notion or any modern block editor — start typing for text, use `/` to open the block picker, drag blocks to reorder.

Common blocks you'll reach for first:

- **Heading** — H1, H2, H3.
- **Text** — paragraphs, lists, quotes, links.
- **Data token** — inline value pulled from a connected integration (e.g., "Total sessions: `{ga4.sessions}`"). Resolves at render time using the reporting period.
- **Table** — for KPI rollups.
- **Chart** — line, bar, or area. Bound to a data source the same way as tokens.
- **Image** — for screenshots or branded assets.
- **Callout** — for highlighting wins, blockers, or asks.

If a data block can't find its number — for example, the chosen metric returned `null` for that period — the editor shows an inline warning so you catch it before send.

## Step 3 (optional): Add sections and assign teammates

For solo briefs, skip this — write top to bottom in the editor and move on.

For team briefs, click **Add section** at the top of the editor. Each section is a named group of blocks with its own status and (optionally) an assignee:

- **Status**: `to do`, `in progress`, `done`, or `additional notes`. Visible to teammates so everyone knows where the brief stands.
- **Assignee**: any workspace Member, Admin, or Owner. They'll get a notification ([if their preferences allow it](/features/notifications/)) when assigned.

The brief as a whole can't move to `pending_approval` until every section is `done` (or marked `additional notes`).

## Step 4: Preview the brief

Click **Preview** at the top of the editor. The brief renders the way the client will see it in their portal — branded chrome, your colors, your logo.

<Placeholder type="screenshot" caption="Preview pane showing the rendered brief with the client's branding" />

What to check:

- **Are the numbers right?** Cross-check at least one data token against the source platform.
- **Does any block look empty or weird?** A zero is fine; a blank is usually a binding issue.
- **Are dates correct?** The period header should match what you set in Step 1.
- **Does the branding match expectations?** Logo, color, recipient name.

You can switch between desktop and mobile preview to make sure nothing breaks on phones.

## Step 5: Submit for approval (if required)

If your workspace requires approval on briefs, click **Submit for approval**. The brief moves to `pending_approval` and assigned approvers get a notification.

Approvers can:

- **Approve** — moves the brief to `approved`. From here you (or the approver) can send.
- **Request changes** — moves the brief to `changes_requested` with a note for the author. The author fixes, resubmits.

Owners and Admins can approve their own briefs (useful for solo agencies). Members typically can't — depends on workspace policy.

If approvals aren't required in your workspace, you'll see **Send** instead of **Submit for approval** at this step.

## Step 6: Send a test to yourself first

Click **Send test to me** (or the equivalent menu option) before the client sees anything. The brief delivers to *your* email so you can:

- See it in your actual inbox (rendering can differ slightly from preview).
- Click through any links to make sure they work, including the portal link.
- Confirm subject line and from-address look right.

This step takes 30 seconds and catches problems the preview pane can't show.

## Step 7: Send to the client

When the test looks right:

1. Click **Send to client**.
2. Confirm the recipient list (every contact on this client with `receive reports` on).
3. Choose **Send now**. Scheduling for later is supported on the brief detail page after creation — see [Monthly client reporting](/flows/monthly-reporting/) for the full schedule-and-forget workflow.
4. Confirm.

The brief moves to `sent`. You'll see a confirmation, and the brief appears in the **Sent** tab on the Reports list.

## Step 8: Watch delivery

Open the sent brief and look at the engagement bar at the top. It shows, per recipient:

- **Delivered** — the email left our system successfully (usually within 30 seconds).
- **Opened** — the recipient loaded the email (best-effort — privacy filters like Apple Mail Privacy Protection mask this).
- **Clicked** — any tracked link was clicked (including the portal link).
- **Portal views** — the client opened the brief in the hosted portal.

Don't read too much into "0 opens" — many email clients block tracking. Portal views are usually a better signal because the recipient has to actively click through.

## You did it

You sent a brief. Every brief after this one is faster — save this one as a template (**Brief menu → Save as template**), and the next client gets a one-click starting point.
