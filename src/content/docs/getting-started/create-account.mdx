## Before you start

You'll need:

- A **work email address** (recommended — see [Why work email matters](#why-work-email-matters) below).
- About **3 minutes**.

You will **not** need:

- A credit card. The trial runs 14 days, no card.
- Your team's invitation list. You can invite them later.
- Your client list. You'll set up your first client in the next step.

## The signup flow

<Placeholder type="screenshot" caption="The /signup page with email, name, and password fields visible" />

1. Go to [app.sendbriefs.com/signup](https://app.sendbriefs.com/signup).
2. Enter your email, your full name, and a password (12+ characters).
3. *Optional*: add your agency name now. You can fill or change it later.
4. Submit. Check your inbox for the verification email.
5. Click the verification link. If it's stale, request a new one from the login screen.
6. You're signed in.

> **Password policy.** We require 12+ characters with at least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, one number, and one special character. We check the password against the [Have I Been Pwned](https://haveibeenpwned.com/) breach database — known-breached passwords are rejected. This is non-negotiable: a reused password protects nothing.

## Why work email matters

Two reasons:

- **Billing and account recovery** route through this email. A personal Gmail you'll lose access to when you change jobs is a bad anchor.
- **Team identification** is easier when colleagues share a domain. Future invitations and workspace-level controls expect a consistent email pattern.

If you signed up with a personal email by mistake, you can change it later from **Settings → Profile**, but it's faster to use the right one from the start.

## Verify your email

Until you click the verification link, your account can read its own settings but can't send briefs. The link lands you back in the app — pick up the onboarding wizard from there.

Didn't get the email? Check spam. If it's truly missing, request a new one from the login page — and check that the email address you typed is correct.

## Turn on two-factor authentication

Highly recommended. You can do it now or after the workspace setup:

1. Open your **Profile** page from the sidebar avatar.
2. Find the **Security** section and click **Enable two-factor authentication**.
3. Scan the QR code with your authenticator app. Anything that supports the standard 6-digit code format (TOTP) works — 1Password, Authy, Google Authenticator.
4. Enter the 6-digit code to confirm.
5. **Save the recovery codes somewhere safe.** If you lose your phone, these are how you get back in.

<Placeholder type="screenshot" caption="TOTP enrollment screen with QR code and recovery codes" />

> **Workspace-enforced MFA.** If your workspace owner has enforced MFA, you'll be required to enroll. The flow is:
>
> 1. The system routes you to `/mfa-enroll` at next login.
> 2. You get a **7-day grace period**. A banner warns you but you can still use the dashboard.
> 3. After 7 days, you're blocked from the dashboard until you enroll.
> 4. The enrollment itself is the same TOTP flow described above.
